About

From
Minsk
to MIT
to Music

"Really shines on the bandstand." — Jon Garelick, jazz critic

Born in Belarus, Mitch showed rare musical gifts before age 8, earning recognition in formal conservatory competitions. His path took an extraordinary detour through academia — earning a Ph.D. in Engineering from MIT — before he returned to music with the full weight of that analytical mind behind his playing.

He studied with two of the most respected guitarists in jazz today: Peter Bernstein and Jonathan Kreisberg. Based in Boston, Mitch leads the ML Jazz Collaborative, bringing a composer's ear and an engineer's precision to music that feels entirely human.

Music

Evolution

The ML Jazz Collaborative

Evolution is the debut full-length from Mitch Livstone's working trio — a record that earns its title. It captures a band that has grown into a shared language: intricate, interactive, and alive to the moment.

With Tim Ray's harmonic depth, Ehud Ettun's lyrical bass, and Gary Fieldman's intuitive pulse, the trio gives Mitch's compositions space to breathe and fire to burn.

The Playing

How he sounds

Lyricism

Every phrase tells a story. Mitch's lines unfold with the inevitability of a well-constructed sentence — nothing wasted, nothing missing.

Orchestration

He plays guitar the way a conductor leads an ensemble — shaping dynamics, leaving space, and bringing every band member into the conversation.

Tone

Brilliant, warm, and malleable. His sound is immediately recognizable — rooted in the jazz tradition while entirely his own.
