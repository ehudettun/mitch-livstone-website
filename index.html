"Really shines on the bandstand." — Jon Garelick · The Boston Globe

Before most guitarists find their first chord, Mitch Livstone was placing ahead of adult conservatory students in his native Minsk. He went on to earn a Ph.D. in Engineering from MIT — and then made the decision that defined everything: to commit, fully and without compromise, to jazz.

He didn't come up quietly. He sought out Peter Bernstein and Jonathan Kreisberg — two of the most celebrated jazz guitarists working today — and studied directly with them. He built his reputation the right way, night after night, on Boston's most important stages. With Evolution, the music has caught up with the reputation.

Music

Evolution

The ML Jazz Collaborative

Evolution isn't simply a debut — it's a declaration. Four originals and three newly-claimed standards, all moving with the confidence of a band that knows exactly what it wants to say, and exactly how to say it.

With Tim Ray's deep harmonic vocabulary, Ehud Ettun's lyrical bass, and Gary Fieldman's intuitive pulse, the ML Jazz Collaborative plays like a single instrument. Released in 2026 to immediate critical attention, Evolution marks Mitch Livstone's arrival on his own terms.

The Playing

What sets him apart

Lyricism

Every phrase carries weight. Mitch's lines unfold with the inevitability of a well-constructed sentence — nothing wasted, nothing missing, nothing arbitrary.

Orchestration

He plays guitar the way a great conductor leads — shaping dynamics, knowing when to step back, and pulling every voice in the room into the same moment.

Tone

Warm, brilliant, immediately recognizable. His sound lives at the intersection of tradition and full originality — and it's unmistakably his.
